Important disclaimer: This benchmark represents my personal experiments with various AI coding assistants, not a rigorous scientific study or official evaluation. The scores and observations are entirely subjective, based on my own criteria and preferences for UI implementation. Additionally, LLMs are non-deterministic by nature — running the same prompt multiple times may produce different outcomes. Different users might reach completely different conclusions based on their priorities. Consider this a snapshot of one developer's experience rather than authoritative guidance on tool selection.

My Impressions

  • GPT-5 tends to favor dark themes, producing smooth animations and seamless page transitions. The overall look is sleek and professional, resembling high-end photo editing software.
  • Grok 4 (Cursor) delivers strong performance in coding the business logic, but its UI design feels unrefined. It gives the impression of being built by a skilled developer with limited attention to visual design principles.
  • In comparing Opus 4.1 and Sonnet 4, the Opus 4.1 output appears more polished and sophisticated, whereas the Sonnet design comes across as simpler and less refined.
  • Surprisingly, Cursor CLI produces very strong results.
